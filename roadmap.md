# Study roadmap

This roadmap defines the Java section inside `my-brain`.

| # | Topic | Goal | Why it matters | Key Java tools/classes | Recommended problems | Suggested order | Difficulty |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 01 | Java Basics | Build confidence with syntax and core language features | Everything else depends on this foundation | `class`, `method`, `String`, `Arrays`, `ArrayList`, `Math` | Small syntax drills, utility methods | 1 | Beginner |
| 02 | Big O | Learn runtime and memory analysis | Helps explain tradeoffs clearly in interviews | Complexity notation, loop counting, recursion analysis | Analyze existing problems | 2 | Beginner |
| 03 | Arrays | Master indexing, traversal, and in-place updates | Arrays appear in many first-round questions | Primitive arrays, `Arrays`, `System.arraycopy` | Two Sum, Contains Duplicate, Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock, Move Zeroes, Product of Array Except Self | 3 | Easy |
| 04 | Strings | Practice scanning and transformation patterns | String questions are common and easy to discuss clearly | `String`, `StringBuilder`, `charAt`, `substring` | Valid Anagram, Valid Palindrome, First Unique Character, Longest Substring Without Repeating Characters | 4 | Easy |
| 05 | HashMap & HashSet | Learn fast lookup and frequency counting | Core pattern for counting and grouping | `HashMap`, `HashSet`, `getOrDefault`, `containsKey` | Two Sum (HashMap), Valid Anagram (frequency map), Group Anagrams, Top K Frequent Elements | 5 | Easy-Medium |
| 06 | ArrayList & Java Collections | Understand dynamic lists and collection helpers | Useful for interview code and production code | `ArrayList`, `List`, `Collections`, `Comparator` | Dynamic Array, Frequency Counter utility | 6 | Easy-Medium |
| 07 | Stack, Queue & Deque | Learn ordered processing and bracket matching | Very common for parsing and monotonic patterns | `ArrayDeque`, `Deque`, `Stack` (legacy), `Queue` | Valid Parentheses, Min Stack | 7 | Easy-Medium |
| 08 | Sorting | Understand ordering and comparison costs | Sorting often simplifies harder problems | `Arrays.sort`, `Collections.sort`, `Comparator` | Sorting practice set | 8 | Easy-Medium |
| 09 | Two Pointers | Solve paired traversal problems efficiently | Great for arrays and strings | Index pointers, `Arrays`, `String` | Pair sums, palindrome-style scans | 9 | Easy-Medium |
| 10 | Sliding Window | Learn fixed and variable window patterns | Strong substring and subarray technique | `HashMap`, `ArrayDeque` | Longest/shortest window exercises | 10 | Medium |
| 11 | Prefix Sum | Turn range queries into constant-time lookups | Useful for subarray sums and ranges | Prefix arrays, running totals | Range sum problems | 11 | Easy-Medium |
| 12 | Binary Search | Search efficiently on sorted or monotonic input | Classic interview pattern | `Arrays.binarySearch`, custom loops | Search and boundary problems | 12 | Medium |
| 13 | Recursion | Build decomposition and base-case discipline | Foundation for trees, backtracking, DP | Call stack, helper methods | Factorial, tree traversal, simple recursion | 13 | Medium |
| 14 | Linked List | Practice pointer manipulation | Common interview representation of nodes | Custom node classes | Reverse list, detect cycles | 14 | Medium |
| 15 | Trees | Learn DFS and BFS on hierarchical data | Very common in interviews | `Queue`, recursion, custom tree nodes | Traversals, depth, path problems | 15 | Medium |
| 16 | Heap & PriorityQueue | Manage top-k and ordering efficiently | Helps with ranking and scheduling | `PriorityQueue`, `Comparator` | Top-k, merge k lists, stream median ideas | 16 | Medium |
| 17 | Graphs | Model connections and traversal | Important for real interview depth | `List`, `Queue`, `Set`, adjacency lists | BFS/DFS connectivity, islands-style problems | 17 | Medium-Hard |
| 18 | Backtracking | Explore decision trees with pruning | Common for combinations and permutations | Recursion, `List`, `Set` | Subsets, permutations, combinations | 18 | Medium-Hard |
| 19 | Dynamic Programming | Reuse subproblem results | A major interview filter topic | Arrays, memoization maps, tables | Climbing stairs, house robber, knapsack-style | 19 | Hard |
| 20 | Bit Manipulation | Work directly with bits and masks | Useful for compact reasoning | Bitwise operators `&`, `|`, `^`, `<<`, `>>` | Single number, bit counting | 20 | Medium |
| 21 | Greedy | Make local choices with proof | Useful when optimal substructure is simple | Sorting, heaps, intervals | Interval scheduling, stock variants | 21 | Medium |
| 22 | Mock Assessments | Practice mixed review under time pressure | Simulates real interview conditions | All prior tools | Mixed-topic review sets | 22 | Mixed |
